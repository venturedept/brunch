How many millennials does it take to screw in a lightbulb?

Three; one to look up “screw in a lightbulb tutorial” on YouTube, one to write a BuzzFeed article about learning to love lightbulbs while you’re still in your twenties, and one to instagram a picture of the new lightbulb with the hashtag #blessed.

The average millennial switches between media platforms 27 times per hour.* That means you’ve got to deliver your message quickly, uniquely, and in a way that speaks directly to their lives. As a creative agency made of up students, The Venture Dept. knows how to talk to millennials. We’d like to share that knowledge with you at an event we’re calling Brunch With Venture.

Here’s what you’ll learn:

1

Keynote: Can a university stay relevant in its students' lives?

Presented by Deb Morrison, Chambers Distinguished Professor of Advertising, UO School of Journalism and Communication

How can we, as participants in and influencers of UO’s campus culture, craft narratives that inspire students to collaborate, grow, and support one another? What happens when students become apathetic towards the messaging and programming of a major university? Is there a way to cut through bureaucracy and tell meaningful stories that resonate with students?

2

“Know thy audience” & other tattoos to consider

In order to talk to millennials, you’ve got to first define what they are. After that, you need to know exactly what they think of you so you can speak to them in an honest way. Millennials will trash your email, unfollow your twitter, or ignore your print ad if it doesn’t interest them, so learning to offer value while telling engaging stories is key to success. Just talking to students sometimes won’t loop you into what they’re interested in; you’ve got to do some sleuthing on your own, and you’ve got to do it efficiently.

3

The New Media Twerkshop: Making your content relevant

Millennials have high expectations about the level of polish in the media they consume. Because your audience is a visually literate generation, you’ve got to find trends in media and latch on to them while students are attracted to them. We’ll specifically look at examples in:

  • Design
  • Video
  • Brand Identity
  • Social media and web presence
  • Alt media and installations
4

Started from the bottom now we here, here, and here

You’ve made a beautiful video that speaks directly to your audience. Now where’s it going to live? A single video on YouTube won’t do anything for your brand without a way for your audience to discover it. Developing strategies to get your media in the hands of millennials is a key piece of every campaign, as well as examining exactly how each of the pieces of your concept fit together.

5

(Translation from emoji to English: How do we put this all together to make something that everyone will love?) Once you understand millennials, how to tailor content to them, and how to get your message at the center of their attention, you’re a valuable ally that The Venture Dept. would love to work with. We’ll walk you through the way that we put all of these pieces together on a daily basis to create our work, so that you can get a holistic look at the whole process.
